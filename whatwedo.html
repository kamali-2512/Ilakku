WHAT WE DO


At ILAKKU, every activity is designed to enrich students’ lives, build confidence, and inspire leadership.We focus on education, skills, health,and awareness,ensuring that first-generation learners not only excel in their studies but also grow into changemakers in their families and communities.









Our Students Education and Career Program

At ILAKKU, we believe that education is the first step to empowerment. Many of our students are first-generation learners, facing challenges like lack of resources, limited family support, or financial struggles. To bridge this gap, we offer academic mentoring, English and computer classes, grammar lessons, and study skill workshops that strengthen their learning foundation.

Beyond academics, we prepare students for the future workplace. Career group meetings, discussions on certificate courses, project-based learning, and career-awareness sessions introduce them to diverse paths like paramedical studies, IT, teaching, and entrepreneurship. Students also engage in resume building, communication practice, and leadership exercises, which boost their confidence to face interviews and professional settings.

Here are the Key focus areas of our work:

Certificate Courses and Skill Building

Through structured certificate programs, students gain essential skills in study methods, digital literacy, and communication. Additional training includes career exploration, project-based learning, and participation in national-level certificate opportunities like NPTEL,broadening their academic and professional horizons.

Residential Programs and Field Visits

We organise leadership retreats, house visits, and exposure programs to connect learning with real life. Residential training builds confidence and teamwork, while house visits deepen empathy by helping students understand the struggles of families and the importance of resilience and education.

Health and Awareness Campaigns

We celebrate important days like International Immunisation Day, hosting sessions with health professionals on anaemia, menstrual hygiene, reproductive health, nutrition, and cancer awareness. Student clubs and Readers’ Club discussions extend this learning, encouraging students to share knowledge with peers and families.


Student Activities and Leadership

Students take the lead through clubs and groups, organising debates, mindfulness activities, and career awareness sessions. They explore topics from paramedical studies to project-based learning, strengthening creativity, teamwork, and leadership while inspiring one another through peer learning.

Parent & Community Engagement

Parents are invited into the journey through regular meetings, where they learn about student progress and discuss ways to support their children. Awareness drives and open discussions highlight the importance of women’s higher education, ensuring the community stands behind the students’ success.
